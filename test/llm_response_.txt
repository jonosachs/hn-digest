      "background": "Processors are built by etching circuits onto silicon using a technology called a \"process node.\" When companies refer to \"18A\" or \"1.8nm,\" they are describing how small and densely packed the transistors are on the chip; smaller transistors are faster and use less power. Intel, an American company that both designs and makes chips, is currently in a race to match the manufacturing precision of TSMC, the Taiwanese company that makes chips for Apple and Nvidia. Because it is difficult to make one giant, perfect piece of silicon, modern chips are made of \"chiplets\"—multiple smaller pieces connected together in one package. Intel uses \"Foveros\" technology to stack these chiplets vertically (like floors in a skyscraper) and \"EMIB\" bridges to connect them horizontally. Inside the processor, \"cache\" is a small amount of extremely fast memory located right next to the cores to store the data they need most often. Without enough cache, cores must wait for data to travel from the main system RAM, which is much slower. By including over 1GB of cache, Intel ensures that all 288 cores can stay busy with work, making the chip much more efficient for massive cloud computing tasks."
